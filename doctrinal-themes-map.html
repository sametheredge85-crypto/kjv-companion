Doctrinal Themes Map

A whole‑counsel overview of Scripture’s major themes and how they interlock. This map helps restore harmony, context, and clarity across the KJV.

1. Time vs Eternity

Eternal (God’s finished work):

  • chosen in Christ
  • redeemed by the Lamb
  • justified by grace
  • loved with everlasting love
  • secured by Christ’s intercession

Timely (our experience):

  • faith
  • repentance
  • obedience
  • assurance
  • growth

Confusing these creates doctrinal chaos. Distinguishing them restores harmony.

2. Flesh vs Spirit

  • flesh: natural, temporal, weak, corruptible
  • spirit: eternal, renewed, enlightened, preserved

Many passages describe conflict, not identity.

3. Life vs Death

  • life: union with Christ, light, understanding
  • death: separation, darkness, blindness

Life is a gift; understanding is an experience.

4. Light vs Darkness

  • light: truth, revelation, clarity
  • darkness: ignorance, confusion, blindness

Walking in light is timely; being made light is eternal.

5. Law vs Grace

  • law: exposes sin, condemns, reveals need
  • grace: saves, teaches, strengthens

Law shows the problem; grace provides the remedy.

6. Discipleship vs Sonship

  • Sonship: eternal, unconditional, finished
  • Discipleship: timely, conditional, practical

Confusing these leads to works‑salvation or fear‑based religion.

7. Israel vs the Nations

Many passages address Israel specifically. Understanding this prevents misapplication.

8. Kingdom vs World

  • kingdom: righteousness, peace, joy, spiritual rule
  • world: systems of men, darkness, corruption

9. Assurance vs Salvation

Salvation: God’s eternal work. Assurance: our timely understanding of it.

10. Experience vs Reality

Reality is what God has done. Experience is how we perceive it.
